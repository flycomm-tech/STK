⚠ Threat Landscape

Classified anomalies by severity and potential threat vector

CRITICAL   Phantom PLMN 416-77

19,347 records (44.5%) broadcast PLMN code 416-77. This code is not registered to any known Jordanian operator. It appears paired with Israeli carrier names (Pelephone, Cellcom, Partner, Golan Telecom) and ISO code "il". Records span the entire geographic territory of Israel — from Eilat (lat 29.5) to the northern border (lat 33.1).

Assessment: An unregistered PLMN broadcasting across an entire country is a signature of either unauthorized cellular infrastructure or a large-scale IMSI-catcher deployment.

CRITICAL   Egyptian MCC in Israeli Territory

153 records with Egyptian MCC 602, including PLMNs 602-10 (Vodafone Egypt), 602-03 (Etisalat), 602-04. These signals appear near Kfar Saba, Petah Tikva, and the central West Bank (lat ~32.01, lon ~35.10) — over 200 km from the Egyptian border. Several records pair Egyptian PLMNs with Israeli operator name "Pelephone" and ISO "il".

Assessment: Egyptian cellular signals cannot physically reach central Israel. This indicates spoofed base stations broadcasting Egyptian network identities.

HIGH   Jordanian PLMNs Deep Inside Israel

Beyond the phantom 416-77, legitimate Jordanian PLMNs 416-01 (Zain) and 416-03 (Umniah) collectively represent 8,719 records. While border-adjacent signals are expected in the Jordan Valley, these PLMNs appear in locations well beyond natural spillover range, including the central coastal plain.

862 records pair these Jordanian PLMNs with Israeli carrier names — a physical impossibility in legitimate roaming.

HIGH   Exotic Foreign MCCs

Signals from Greece (202-05, 147 records), Spain (214-01, 24 records), Saudi Arabia (420-04, 31 records), and even Iran (432-11, 1 record) were detected. None of these countries share a border with Israel.

Assessment: These cannot be explained by legitimate signal spillover and suggest either rogue base stations or testing of spoofed identities.

MEDIUM   MCC-000 and PLMN 255-255

10,694 records with test/invalid PLMNs (000-00 and 255-255). While often associated with unconfigured equipment, the volume and geographic spread suggest cellular infrastructure operating outside regulatory compliance. These transmitters broadcast without proper network identification.

⚙ Detection Methodology

Multi-layer forensic analysis pipeline

Layer 1 — MCC/PLMN Validation

Each signal's PLMN is validated against the ITU MCC registry. Any non-Israeli MCC (non-425) inside Israeli territory is flagged. PLMNs are cross-referenced against known operator databases.

Layer 2 — Operator-PLMN Cross Matching

Operator names are mapped to expected country origins. An Israeli operator name (Pelephone, Partner, Cellcom) paired with a foreign PLMN is a direct IMSI-catcher indicator.

Layer 3 — Geographic Feasibility

Signal locations are compared against the broadcasting country's border. Signals from Egypt detected 200km inside Israel cannot be legitimate spillover (typical range: 10-30km).

Layer 4 — Temporal Burst Analysis

Anomalous signals are analyzed for temporal clustering. Multiple foreign PLMNs appearing in the same location within short time windows suggest active interception operations.

Layer 5 — AI-Powered Threat Assessment

Machine learning models cross-correlate all layers to produce a composite threat score. The AI analyst generates per-cluster risk assessments with actionable intelligence.
